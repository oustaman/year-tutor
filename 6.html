This webpage is a mini list with some resources to help you get on with your personal tutoring. For suggestions, changes, corrections, or dead links, contact me at p.jagodzinski@mmu.ac.uk.

Please note: The checkboxes may reset when you refresh the page and aren't saved. They are there to indicate this is a task that you can do and offer you the satisfaction of ticking off a checkbox. No data is collected, and nobody would know if you ticked the boxes or not.

Email Templates

Suggested check-in email
Hi there!

I hope you’re well and settling in to university life this academic year.

As your Personal Tutor, I’m just checking in to see if you’re OK and to help you with any concerns or questions.

Either

it looks like you missed x session this week. It’s important to attend all timetabled sessions and to let us know when you’re going to be absent. You can report an absence (e.g. illness) online via this link. If you are having problems attending sessions, please let me know so that we can work out how to help.

OR

It looks like you attended all your timetable sessions this week which is a great start! How’s it going?

Later in Block 1, we’ll have the first of our regular 5 year plan meetings so look out for an email from me inviting you to join me either in person or via Teams.

Take care!
(Your name)

Invitation to 1st meeting
Hi there!

I've scheduled our next Personal Tutoring meeting for X. Please 'accept' the meeting and use the Teams link below to join. If you're unable to attend, please let me know and suggest an alternative time in the same week. It's important that we have this regular contact.

In advance of our meeting, I'd like you to spend some time on a Self-Assessment Activity.

This isn't a formal test! It's just an opportunity for you to reflect and for us to discuss how to best support you in your professional plans and career development.

Take care!
(Your name)

Hello student name!

Thank you for participating in our Personal Tutoring meetings so far. This is an invitation to our last meeting this year. I have scheduled it for X. Please 'accept' the meeting and use the Teams link below to join. If you're unable to attend, please let me know and suggest an alternative time in the same week. It's important that we have this regular contact.

In advance or our meeting I’d like you to reflect about the process so far and think about how it can benefit your future professional trajectory. You can also think about any further questions you might have in relation to your plan as well as additional resources you might need.

Take care,
(Your name)
